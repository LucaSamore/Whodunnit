## Retrospective
Dedicating a full sprint to testing and documentation proved to be the right decision for ensuring project quality and completeness. 
The systematic approach to documentation, with clear assignment of sections among team members, enabled efficient parallel work and timely completion. 
The testing phase for the Solution submission feature validated the robustness of the implementation and confirmed the effectiveness of the TDD and BDD-style approach adopted throughout the project.
Reflecting on the overall development process, the team acknowledged that while backend logic received significant attention and benefited from advanced design patterns, the user interface could have been better organized. The view code, particularly the coupling with ScalaFX and the mixing of container scenes with components, represents an area for improvement in future iterations.
The project successfully delivered all core functionalities, but this sprint also highlighted the cost of certain architectural choices. The Cake Pattern, while providing type-safe dependency injection, introduced complexity that impacted development speed. The lesson learned is that balancing technical sophistication with practical maintainability is crucial. Despite these challenges, the strong collaboration, clear task ownership, and agile process enabled the team to deliver a complete, functional product ready for delivery.
